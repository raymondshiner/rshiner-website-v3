## The portfolio is the proof

For years, the dev portfolio formula was "list your projects, list your stack, throw in a contact form." That formula breaks the moment AI eats the medium.

If anyone can scaffold a slick portfolio in a weekend with Claude or Cursor, the artifact stops being interesting. **The interesting thing becomes the workflow** — the agents you've configured, the prompts you've tuned, the way you've made the AI part of your craft, not a replacement for it.

So when I rebuilt my own portfolio, I made the workflow visible.

## Three things changed

1. **The Workflow section is non-negotiable.** Not "I use Claude" — show the actual subagent files, the actual config snippets, the actual setup. The repo is linked. You can clone it.
2. **The chat is the resume.** Reading bullet points is for ATS robots. A visitor with five minutes and a real question gets a real answer — typed in plain English to an AI that has my resume cached as system context.
3. **The site itself was vibe-coded.** Built in a session with the same tools I'd use on client work. The case study walks through the decisions. The aesthetic was a one-line `AskUserQuestion`. That's not a confession — it's the point.

## What I'm still figuring out

How honest to be about the AI parts. Too little, and it reads like every other portfolio. Too much, and it reads like an ad for Claude. The version live right now is my current best guess.

More notes coming as I keep building.
